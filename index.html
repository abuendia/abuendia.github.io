Hello! I am a computational research lab manager in Raul Rabadan's Lab at Columbia University Irving Medical Center. Previously, I was a research engineer in the Clinical Machine Learning Group at MIT, and a data scientist at Microsoft New England for three years. I completed my bachelor's degree at Columbia University in mathematics and computer science in 2019, during which I was fortunate to be advised by Daniel Hsu. At MIT, I worked on clinical natural language processing and modeling patient trajectories in the electronic health record. While at Microsoft, I worked on predictive features for Office and a financial natural language processing platform for Azure.
I am interested in computational genomics and the development of foundation models for biological applications, particularly on DNA sequences and epigenomic data. In my current position, I am working on deep learning methods to predict the mutational evolution of viral protein sequences, in particular SARS-CoV-2 and influenza.
I am also particularly passionate about diversity and inclusion in the tech, AI, and medical communities, both in the deployment of systems for patients and on the side of developers and researchers. At Microsoft New England, I co-founded and co-led a Diversity and Inclusion intiative with Ehi Nosakhare targeting the recruitment of underrepresented minorities for AI positions in the AI Development Acceleration Program.
